Peer Review: Concept and Applications

What is Peer Review?

Peer review is considered the gate of your paper to the society. This is where your work gets admitted to be introduced to the scientific society or not. Basically, peer review is a process that ensures the quality of your work before it is published. This is the most non-biased gate pf publication followed by the scientific society, thus make sure your work is well-valued.

How does it work?

When you submit your manuscript/scientific paper to a journal, the editorial team at first makes sure that your work meets the criteria and the formatting of the papers that gets published in their journal. After this step, and if your paper met their criteria, the editorial team would assign a reviewer or more than one (based on the journal‚Äôs capacity) in order to fully review your manuscript, ensuring its originality, validity, and significance to the scientific society.

Open peer review

Reviewers statusAuthors statusTrade offs
Know who the authors are.Know who the reviewers are.1) Enhance the reviewer accountability towards the reviewing process. 2) Develop a mode of transparency between the reviewer and the author, besides the mode of the constructive criticism that invades the consideration of the reviewer throughout his/her work. 3) Less scholarly rigor throughout the process. 4) May result in unreasonable conflicts regarding the work throughout the field itself.

Why do peer review?

Peer review is an integral block in your research journey that validates your work via experts in the field, who do all make sure that your final published work came to be:

  • More Robust with no gaps in the topic you do propose, and a complete clarity for every part of your work.
  • Readable and easy to be accessed via a wide range of those interested in the field you are writing about.
  • Of high quality regarding the proposed data, methodology, and findings in a way that respects the readers mindsets.

Peer review is an essential step that you will go through if you decide to submit your work for publication in a journal. Be sure to track the type of peer review, understand the process, and keep a record of the peer-review steps. This will help you avoid wasting time after investing significant effort in the publication journey.
