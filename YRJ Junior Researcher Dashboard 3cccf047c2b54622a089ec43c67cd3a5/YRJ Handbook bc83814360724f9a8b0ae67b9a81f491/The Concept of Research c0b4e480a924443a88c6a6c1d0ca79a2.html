The Concept of Research

For someone who genuinely loves research, just like us, it represents progress in human history. It lies deep at the heart of innovation and knowledge advancement. It is a quest for understanding, a journey of exploration that seeks to uncover new insights, solve problems, and contribute to the body of knowledge. One main purpose of research is to fill the wide gap between what we know and what we don’t. It’s the vehicle we ride to acquire knowledge and add to academic disciplines.

Characteristics of Research

Before getting into how one can be involved in the process, let us take a look at the main characteristics of research. Research is characterized by some particular attributes that set it apart from casual observation or anecdotal information. Those characteristics ensure that research is done in a systematic, objective, and rigorous manner.

I. Systematic Nature

Without mentioning so much detail here, research follows a structured and organized process that involves clearly defined steps and procedures. It begins with the identification of a research question or objective, followed by the formulation of a research plan, data collection, analysis, interpretation, and finally, the dissemination of findings. This systematic approach ensures that research is conducted methodically and logically, enhancing the reliability of results.

II. Objectivity

In simple words, research has so much integrity in itself. Researchers always aim to approach their investigations without any personal biases or preconceived notions. They aim to gather and analyze data objectively, allowing the evidence, along with nothing else, to guide their conclusions.

III. Generation of New Knowledge

It aims to contribute to the existing body of knowledge by uncovering new insights, theories, or applications. Through their investigations, researchers seek to advance understanding, pushing the boundaries of knowledge.

IV. Cumulative Nature of Research

It builds upon existing knowledge and is often conducted within the context of previous studies. By building upon previous research, researchers can establish continuity, draw comparisons, and identify areas that require further exploration.

Types of Research:

Now, do you remember our first talk, yes, the one where you are supposed to write a research paper? You asked a very solid question, should I write a research paper or another type of paper? Well, in YRJ, you can write whatever you can since it is accessible, relevant, and ethical. So, what are the different types of research?

I. Experimental Research

It is where researchers manipulate variables and observe their effects to establish cause-and-effect relationships. Experimental research often involves statistical analysis to draw conclusions. Although this type of research is used in almost every field, it is more common in physics, biology, and chemistry.

II. Observational Research

It is where researchers observe and document phenomena without intervening or manipulating variables. Observational research can be useful in studying natural behaviors, social interactions, or complex systems. It also involves the analysis of data.

III. Survey Research

It involves data collection through questionnaires or interviews. Surveys allow researchers to gather information from a large number of participants and explore attitudes, opinions, or behaviors. Data from surveys can be analyzed quantitatively or qualitatively, depending on the particular research objectives. This type of research is mainly used in the social sciences, mathematics, humanities, computer science, and business.

IV. Qualitative Research

Qualitative research focuses on exploring and understanding the experiences, perceptions, or meanings associated with a particular phenomenon. The collected data is then analyzed using statistical analysis or other qualitative techniques.

V. Quantitative Research

Quantitative research involves the collection and analysis of numerical data. It relies on statistical methods to identify patterns, relationships, or trends.

Types of Articles

Now, we will be discussing different types of articles.

I. Research Article

Research articles present original research findings and represent empirical research. They contribute to the body of knowledge by introducing new discoveries, methodologies, or insights.

II. Review Article

It provides a comprehensive overview and analysis of existing research on a specific topic. Review articles critically evaluate the existing literature, identify gaps or controversies, and propose future research directions. However, they aren’t made for the sake of presenting new findings. These types of articles help readers gain a deeper understanding of a particular field or topic by synthesizing and summarizing relevant studies.

Note that there are other article types: case reports, conference papers, technical reports, etc.

The Research Process

Now, you got some essence of what the concept means. However, you still miss a really big part: the research process. To make it easier to grasp, let’s talk about science first.

The scientific method, or scientific approach, is the process of objectively establishing facts through testing and experimentation. It is based on making a hypothesis, which is a testable statement about the relationship between two or more variables or a proposed explanation for some observed phenomenon, and trying to prove that hypothesis through experimental data. The last step of the scientific method is communicating your final findings to the scientific community for criticism. This step right there is the one including further thought on how you could correctly pass down the information you gained to descending generations of scientists in the field.

All in all, the research process is the systematic manner in which a researcher approaches their area of study to produce knowledge that the community will consider worthwhile within the field. Research is done through the following steps:

I. Identifying Research Questions

The research process commences with identifying clear and focused research questions that guide the study. These questions define the scope and objectives of the research.

II. Literature Review

Researchers conduct a comprehensive literature review to understand existing knowledge, identify gaps, and establish a theoretical framework for their study. It ensures awareness of previous research, methodologies used, and potential limitations or controversies in the field.

III. Designing the Study

In this stage, researchers determine the appropriate research design, methodology, and data collection techniques for their study. You should focus that the research design should align with the research questions and objectives.

IV. Data Collection

The collection involves gathering relevant information or evidence to answer the research questions. Researchers employ various methods to collect data, such as surveys, interviews, experiments, or observations.

V. Data Analysis

Once the data is collected, researchers analyze it using appropriate statistical or qualitative analysis methods. Quantitative data may be analyzed using statistical software to identify patterns, correlations, or trends. Qualitative data may involve coding and categorizing themes.

VI. Interpretation and Reporting

In this stage, researchers interpret the analyzed data, draw conclusions, and discuss the implications of their findings. They compare their results with existing literature, address research questions, and present a coherent narrative. Researchers may use tables, figures, or visual representations to illustrate their findings.

All in all, research is an invaluable tool that humanity has been utilizing since the dawn of history. Now, you are familiar with its nature, core characteristics, types, and research process.
