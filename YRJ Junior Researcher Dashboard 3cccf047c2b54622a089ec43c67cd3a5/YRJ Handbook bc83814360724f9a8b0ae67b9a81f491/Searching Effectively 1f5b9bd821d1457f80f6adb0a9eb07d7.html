Narrowing Down Results with Boolean Operators

While you are researching, you will find it handy to effectively narrow down your results. Here comes the use of Boolean operators. These operators are used to either narrow or broaden your set of results. The three main Boolean operators are: AND, OR, and NOT.

Introduction to Boolean Operators

First, AND is used to narrow your research results. It tells the databases that all search terms must exist in the results. On the other hand, the use of OR broadens your results. It tells the databases that any of your search terms can be present. Finally, NOT excludes terms from your search. It narrows your research by telling the database to ignore the concepts implied by your terms.

Ethical Considerations in Resource Acquisition

A common mistake that you must be aware of is using illegal resources to get your research articles. Here in YRJ, we do not encourage the usage of pirating sites to find research papers. The usage of those sources may lead to potential threats. Moreover, you can use an extension like ‚ÄúUnpaywall.‚Äù If you opened a webpage including a paid research paper, the extension will tell you if the paper is available freely in other locations. In addition, it tells you if there are no free versions. Another handy way is to type [the paper name] + pdf on the web, and you will probably find it. However, make sure the resource respects the copyrights.
