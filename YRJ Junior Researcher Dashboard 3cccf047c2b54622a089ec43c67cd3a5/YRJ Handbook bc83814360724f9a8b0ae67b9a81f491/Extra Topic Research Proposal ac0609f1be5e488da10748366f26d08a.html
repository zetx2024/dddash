Extra Topic: Research Proposal

Research proposals are simply structured, formal documents that are written in a persuasive style; your research proposal should basically be designed to convince your mentor about the topic you have chosen and its validity to be searched with worthy expected findings, alongside with answering three main questions:

  • What are you planning to investigate?, show more about your plan to delve deeper into your research topic, identifying a draft of your timeline, a list of beneficial resources you plan to rely on, etc (Stay concise).
  • Why should this topic specifically be investigated throughout your study?, simply be reasonable about the justifications of your chosen topic.
  • How would you approach the right investigation methodology throughout your research journey surfing this topic?

This is the most valuable section and part of your research journey to consume time on. In this section, you are required to be a skillful merchant, able to market your ideas to whoever you are proposing your sketch for, specifically your YRJ‚Äôs mentor.

Checkpoints to include in your Proposal:

Detailed explanation for your Research topic

  • Articulate clearly your research topic, consider specificity and unambiguousnes in your presentation for the ideas.
  • Present clearly what you are willing to investigate on the long term, sub-sitting your work to a specific context.
  • Outline your research objectives and research question drafts.

Mini Literature Review

To have a successful proposal, you not only declare a research topic, but also you should make sure of its originality, importance and value to the real world, and efficiency in revealing something considerable in the field you are investigating.

Basically, discuss the gaps your research is aiming at filling in the current researches found in the same field and why should the reader later consider surfing your paper not another one? (Propose your fresh arguments on the topic).

Recommended methodology

The originality of your research topic is never convincing on its own, it requires supplying practicalities that are persuasive as well. This does never mean that at this stage you are required to identify in detail every step in your research methodology, however you are just asked to show a reasonable outline that is well-planned to keep track of throughout your journey towards the previously identified findings out of the process. In other words, you should be able to ask these guidance questions at your proposal:

  • What is exactly your research methodology?
  • How do you make sure they would lead you to the desired findings out of your research topic?
  • Are you considering the major road blocks (Funds, time, field experience, etc.)?

Besides these main points, you are still in need to convince your mentor even more via your proposal before heading towards any developed step further, the reason why you should utilize the guidance of these essential questions, having clear, appropriate answers to them:

  • Which method type approach are you pursuing to follow up with (Qualitative, Quantitative, etc.)?
  • Are you going to run your own experiment or you are just analyzing existing data (which is no less important than the experiment if well-managed)?
  • How will you collect your data (Questionnaires, surveys, etc.)?
  • How will you analyze your data (descriptive and inferential statistics, content analysis, discourse analysis, etc.)?
Before typing any letter in your proposal, give some thought to the practicalities of your research and at least be on a primitive steady methodological plan. And always make sure to be flexible at this time specifically, set your plan A, plan B, etc. in order not to restart everything from scratch every time something fails.
Do never underestimate highlighting the Potential Limitations section to your mentor as early as possible, in order to have the ability to discuss the possibilities of tackling these limitations while progressing in your journey with him/her.
