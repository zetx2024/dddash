Abstract VS Introduction VS Conclusion

What Are the Differences Between Each of Them?

Abstract

The abstract should be designed to stand alone as a separate entity, with no need for the reader to investigate any other parts of your paper. A structured abstract should basically include a background of your research, your study objectives, the methodology attained in your study, the results out of this methodology, and a final comprehensive conclusion.

Introduction

The introduction is the first actual section of your paper; it never should be a repetition for the content of the abstract. It’s more in-depth information about the background of your study, without including any of your methodology, results, or conclusion. It also explains your hypothesis/research question, what you want to discover, why should your study be considered important to this field of science, or what gaps are you trying to fill in the field of your study.

Conclusion

This 1-2 paragraph section is seeking the zoom out of your topic. It basically defines the last touches that should swipe out any misconceptions the reader may develop after reading your work. It should state questions like Why are your results important to be considered? How can your identified scientific field get the ideal benefits out of your findings? Basically, you cover up the generalized idea out of your previously specific one discussed throughout your entire paper sections.
