Research Paper

General Guidelines (MANDATORY)

  • The total word count for the paper must not fall below 3500 words; submissions with fewer words will not be considered.
  • Utilize our provided Microsoft Word template; LaTeX templates will not be considered for acceptance.
  • Ensure your manuscript contains the text, figures, and tables. Upload the complete manuscript, a text-only version, and a figures-only version separately.
  • Figures must have clear captions and should be referenced in the text.
  • All submitted files must be in the .docx format, as specified. Submissions in other formats like PDF or .pages will not be accepted. This uniform format ensures consistency and ease of review.
  • Submit all papers in English language only.
  • Both in-text citations and a comprehensive citations list are mandatory using the IEEE style. Every in-text citation must correspond to an entry in the citations list, and vice versa.

Submission of Versions

  1. When submitting your research paper, please ensure the following:

    Complete Manuscript: Upload the complete manuscript in the .docx format, including all text, figures, and tables. This version should represent the entirety of your research paper.
  1. Text-Only Version: In addition to the complete manuscript, provide a separate text-only version of your paper. This version should contain only the textual content of your paper, excluding any figures or tables.
  1. Figures-Only Version: Similarly, submit a separate figures-only version of your paper. This version should include only the figures and tables, without any accompanying text.

Title Page (Maximum Word Count: 20 words)

  • Craft a clear and succinct title that reflects the core focus of your research.
  • List all authors' names, along with their respective contributions and affiliation details.
  • Give credit to mentors or collaborators who have significantly contributed to your research.

Abstract (Maximum Word Count: 300 words)

  • Provide a concise overview of your research's significance.
  • Define the research problem or objective you're addressing.
  • Outline your research methodology.
  • Summarize the key findings and their implications.
  • Conclude by highlighting the broader impact of your study.

Introduction

  • Set the stage by explaining the context and relevance of your research.
  • Incorporate relevant literature to identify gaps or debates in the field.
  • Clearly articulate the research problem, objectives, and research questions/hypotheses.
  • Offer a brief preview of the major conclusions your study will present.

Literature Review

  • Conduct a comprehensive review of relevant literature to position your research.
  • Highlight the theoretical frameworks guiding your study.
  • Identify the key concepts, theories, and models shaping your research.
  • Emphasize how your study contributes to the existing knowledge.

Methods

  • Describe your research design and methodology in detail.
  • Explain the reasoning behind your sampling strategy and justify the chosen sample size.
  • Detail the methods you used for data collection (surveys, experiments, observations, etc.).
  • Provide insights into the data analysis methods, including the statistical tools employed.

Results

  • Present your research findings in a clear and objective manner.
  • Enhance understanding by utilizing tables, figures, and charts to visualize data effectively.
  • Include both descriptive and inferential statistics as relevant.
  • Ensure that all visual aids are appropriately labeled with clear captions.

Discussion

  • Interpret and analyze your research findings in relation to your research questions/hypotheses.
  • Compare your results with the existing literature, discussing any confirmations or contradictions.
  • Address potential limitations and sources of bias in your study.
  • Explore the theoretical and practical implications of your findings.

Conclusion

  • Summarize the main outcomes of your research concisely.
  • Reinforce the significance and contributions of your study to the field.
  • Offer insights into potential directions for future research based on identified gaps.

References

  • Cite sources using the IEEE citation style. Every citation in the references list must have a corresponding in-text citation, and vice versa.
